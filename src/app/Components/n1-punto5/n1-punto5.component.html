VOCABULARY 2

Person

A man, woman, or child.

  • She is a very nice person.
  • You are the only person I like here.
  • Rooms cost 70 pounds per person.
People

Used to refer to everyone, or informally to the group that you are speaking to.

  • How many people live in England.
  • You can meetamazing people at that festival.
  • Thousans of people marched in protest.
  • Many people arrived before 8 PM.
Group

A number of people or things that are put together or considered as a unit.

  • My group is called 'the Fire'.
  • She is the best student in the group.
  • Each group should consist of 10 people.
  • He is a member of a political group.
Part

Some but not all of a thing.

  • The top part of the shoe is made of leather.
  • You're parte of the family.
  • Travelling is a part of my job.
  • When one part of your car breaks down ypu should see a mechanic.
Government

The group of people who officially control a country.

  • The government consists of three political parties.
  • The government should employ more experts.
  • The government reduced taxes.
Come

To move or travel toward the speaker or with the speaker.

  • Will you come to the party?
  • You can come with your boyfriend.
  • Come home as soon posible.
World

The earth and all the people, places, and things on it.

  • My children are my whole world.
  • I want to travel all over the world.
  • W need to take care of our world.
Time

The part of existence that is measured in minutes, days, years, etc.

  • What time is it?
  • Hurry up, we have no time!
  • He wants to spend more time with his family.
Try

To attempt to do something:.

  • You have to try it!.
  • I am afraid to try this cake, it looks awful!
  • Did ypu try to talk him?
  • I decided to try writing crime stories.
