VOCABULARY 2

Person

A man, woman, or child.

  • She is a very nice person.
  • You are the only person I like here.
  • Rooms cost 70 pounds per person.
People

Used to refer to everyone, or informally to the group that you are speaking to.

  • How many people live in England.
  • You can meetamazing people at that festival.
  • Thousans of people marched in protest.
  • Many people arrived before 8 PM.
Group

A number of people or things that are put together or considered as a unit.

  • My group is called 'the Fire'.
  • She is the best student in the group.
  • Each group should consist of 10 people.
  • He is a member of a political group.
Part

Some but not all of a thing.

  • The top part of the shoe is made of leather.
  • You're parte of the family.
  • Travelling is a part of my job.
  • When one part of your car breaks down ypu should see a mechanic.
City

Is an area in which a large number of people live fairly close together.

  • David is a city located in the west of Panama.
  • The city was invaded and destroyed by pirates in 1642.
T-shirt

A simple piece of clothing, usually with short sleeves and no collar, that covers the top part of the body.

  • She was wearing jeans and a T-shirt.
  • I was wearing a T-shirt under my jumper.
Headache

A pain you feel inside your head.

  • It's an extremely effective cure for a headache.
  • I had a terrible headache, but even so I went to the concert.
Find

To discover.

  • I can't find my keys.
  • I couldn't find Andrew's phone number.
  • I want to find the answers to all these questions.
Neighbour

Someone who lives very close to you.

  • I've asked my neighbour to water the plants while I'm away.
  • Have you met Pat, my next-door neighbour?
